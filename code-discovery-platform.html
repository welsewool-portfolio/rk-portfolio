Code Discovery Platform

Brand-new product for Vendors who provide customers with high-fashioned interior products built on top of a portal for Designers

My Role & Responsibilities

My Role

Product Designer

I undertook the responsibility of overhauling the design process and implementing new frameworks through a series of workshops and activities. Strategic objective was to establish a Design Department and set up robust ecosystem.

My Responsibilities

  • Discovery frameworks
  • Reorganising and setting up design process
  • Establish design department as an entity
  • Prepare design ecosystem
  • Research
  • Scenarios preparation
  • Products & functionality mapping
  • UI execution & concepting
  • Product release preparations
  • Branding

The Challenge

The new Code Discovery Platform is about to hit the market with new vision and strategy. The main objective is to become a market leader and set up a new paradigm for development teams of how to search code and delivery faster. The Product needed the proper ecosystem, processes and concept to be released.

The goal

The primary objective was to establish the Design Department as a fully operational unit, complete with all requisite foundational processes, and execute the first product release along with its branding.

The strategic goal was to launch the initial product along with all necessary satellites to elicit feedback and evolve the ecosystem, which should be thoughtfully prepared and flexible enough.

How I got there

Firstly, I conducted an audit of all the materials produced by the team and those available from open sources. Then I put some efforts to organize the current knowledge into a systematic and coherent path that highlighted the relationships and dependencies between different parts of it. To achieve this, I conducted numerous synchronizing sessions with the team and a series of workshops and interviews to clean up the information and prioritize items.

Next, I engaged the team to structure the vision and impactful pieces of information into an instructional framework that would be visible and clear to every team member, thereby ensuring synchronization of knowledge. Through regular workshops, I mapped the expected system architecture, collected tons of internal feedback, created a powerful brand background, and established a comprehensive UI execution process and environment.

After all the necessary preparation, I executed the first release UI and initial Design System of the Product.

As a UX designer, my efforts were directed towards ensuring that the Product was user-friendly, functional, and aesthetically appealing to the target audience.

Discovery Framework

To properly proceed with the Product Strategy and understand how to set up the collaboration process, I had to review the current state of knowledge synchronization, understand the vision and mission, clarify what is known and what is unknown, gain a sense of how the team works, and how people process input and make decisions. For that purpose, I organized a framework to transfer all the information in an easy-to-understand format.

Deliverables:
  • Discovery framework
  • Roadmaps
  • Service mapping

Personas

In order to effectively address the needs of the Product and Marketing teams, I conducted thorough research and facilitated a workshop with stakeholders to identify and target our audience. This process resulted in the creation of several main groups of personas, for which I developed detailed user persona profiles.

Deliverables:
  • UX Personas
  • Marketing Personas

Use Cases

Through collaborative effort, I identified and captured a comprehensive set of use cases that aligned with the product's intended purpose and addressed the needs and expectations of our users. These use cases will serve as a valuable reference throughout the design and development process, ensuring that we deliver a user-centered and effective solution.

Deliverables:
  • Use cases
  • Categorised list for cross-functional teams

User Stories

I created concise and actionable user stories based on identified use cases, collaborating closely with the project team and stakeholders. These stories reflect key personas and scenarios and will guide the development team in creating a user-centred and effective product.

I also applied affinity mapping technique to organise and structurise stories into logical functional groups.

Deliverables:
  • User stories
  • Affinity maps

Initiatives & Functionality

I developed a comprehensive set of initiatives and functionality groups based on the user stories we identified. These initiatives and groups are designed to address the needs and expectations of the product's target users and ensure a seamless and effective user experience.

Deliverables:
  • Initiatives lists and groups
  • Functional groups
  • Specific functionality priorities

Branding

I led a branding workshop in close collaboration with key team members and stakeholders. Together, we defined the brand's target demographics, visual language, color palette, typography, and tone of voice.

By conducting this branding workshop, we were able to create a strong and cohesive brand identity that effectively communicates the product's unique value proposition and resonates with the target audience.

Deliverables:
  • Visual language
  • Color palette
  • Tone of voice

Products Ecosystem

I mapped the product ecosystem and created mind maps and structures for every main subproduct. Through this process, I gained a comprehensive understanding of how the products interacted with each other and with the user.

To create these mind maps and structures, I worked closely with the project team and stakeholders to identify the key features and functionalities of each subproduct.

Deliverables:
  • Products mindmaps

Viable Version

I also mapped a viable version of products in greater detail. This involved analyzing user feedback, testing different design iterations, and refining the product's features and functionalities based on user needs and preferences.

The resulting detailed mapping of the viable version will serve as a valuable reference throughout the development process, ensuring that the final product is intuitive, user-friendly, and effective.

Deliverables:
  • Mindmaps

Release Map

I created a shortened mind map that focused specifically on the functionality that was planned for release.

This mind map helped to prioritize the features and functionalities that were most critical for the initial release of the product, ensuring that we could deliver a minimum viable product (MVP) that met the needs of our users and aligned with the product's goals and messaging.

Deliverables:
  • Release mindmaps

Execution Plan

During meetings with the project team, I also created a consecutive product release execution plan. This plan detailed the specific steps and timelines for each subsequent release of the product, ensuring that we could deliver new features and functionality in a timely and efficient manner.

Deliverables:
  • Product release execution plan
