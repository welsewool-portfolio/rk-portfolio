The Challenge

MotoShare has started as a small product of few people who passionate about motorcycle riding.
It was created with limited efforts, small team, and weak service design. The first version of the Product had a lot of usability and consistency issues and as a result bad conversion rates and limited growth potential.

The goal

The main idea was making a full redesign of the user interface and improve user experience changing mostly front-end rather than backend.

We hypothesised that usability improvements will improve the conversion rate and as a result will help the product to grow faster.

How I got there

In the beginning, I got onboarding to all business processes which were captured with the flow diagrams. After that, I did the research of the existing solution and its user experience talking to real users through surveys and interviews.

Then I conducted numerous task prioritisation, ideation, and brainstorming sessions on how we can improve UX without big efforts and with minimal changes in the backend.

Next, I did sketches and a few alternative versions of wireframes and after stakeholders' feedback, I accomplished the whole pack of redesigned and improved screens for both desktop and mobile versions.

A/B Testing

During mockups preparation, a lot of different sections was designed with few options to be tested. This could highlight better approaches which provide maximum CTR and build better visual hierarchy.

How I validated Ideas & Solutions

After discussions with the team, I decided to learn more about changes in our main success metric (Conversion rate) and how our new heatmaps look like. I tried to discover whether we set the right visual hierarchy and every main action is clear and clear for users. Also, I conducted numerous feedback sessions with known users so we made sure the changes improved the Product.

Many A/B tests helped us to choose the best UI option for different modules.

Deliverables:
  • Heatmaps (Hotjar)
  • Analytics (Google and others)
  • Feedback sessions notes and prioritisation
  • A/B test modules variety

The Result

After the redesign roll-out, we measured the conversions for every major flow, such as renting out a motorcycle, booking the vehicle, payments, etc.

The redesign returned excellent results and highlighted weak points so we started to investigate core problems in particular places.
