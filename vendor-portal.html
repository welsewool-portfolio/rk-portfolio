Vendor Portal

Brand-new product for Vendors who provide customers with high-fashioned interior products built on top of a portal for Designers

My Role & Responsibilities

My Role

Lead User Experience Designer

I had to validate the idea and create a minimum viable product.

My Responsibilities

  • User research
  • Requirements clarification & creation
  • Finding new product opportunities & features
  • Personas creation
  • Wireframing
  • Visual research
  • User interface design

The Challenge

Vendor Portal was an unclear idea of owners who had already created a portal for designers. So the biggest challenge was shaping an abstract idea and setting the initial requirements in the best way, within a limited timeline and effort.

The goal

The main goal was to execute MVP which is going to cover known problems of vendors, examine the market's opportunity, receive real feedback from potential users, and transform the system to a big environment for both providers and consumers.

The hypothesis was that new product would substitute numerous tools vendors use on an everyday basis.

How I got there

I started with the research of designers' systems striving to understand the data structure they use and the tasks they perform. I conducted 10 user interviews for both designers and vendors to understand the real experience and match it with initial product requirements and strategy. I created scripts, conducted interview sessions, and did a debrief, highlighting all the opportunities and pain points.

Next, I created key personas, converted gathered information into functionality requirements, and mapped them into a mind map. Later, I did a sitemap, created sketches, wireframes, conducted feedback sessions, created a mood board to implement the right style into UI, and as a result executed final mockups for all required flows.

User Interviews

I started with user research to know more details and get insights.

I prepared interview scripts and conducted 10 interviews for both designers and vendors. Next, I did a full debrief of the answers we received.

All the insights, pain points, and opportunities were marked accordingly.

Deliverables:
  • Interview scripts
  • Interview recordings
  • Interview debriefing
  • Interview summary report (Insights / pain points / opportunities)

Insights

I gathered all the insights and research summary, so together with stakeholders, we were able to discuss our strategy.

All the selected ideas were prioritised and put into the product map accordingly.

Deliverables:
  • Presentation
  • Priorities matrix

Personas

For better understanding and segmenting our core users, I created a few user personas, representing our Designers and Vendors. It helped the team and stakeholders better understand how to improve the product itself, it's marketing, and some core business activities they use to satisfy the clients.

Deliverables:
  • Designer user persona
  • Vendor user persona

Information Architecture

Sitemap

Playing with information architecture and functionality maps I created the Sitemap of the future solution. Such an activity helps to see the whole picture, better estimate the delivery time and do not miss important pages, states, and interactive modules. Also, it helps to deliver the product design partially fitting the sprints schedule.

Deliverables:
  • Sitemap
  • UI Design checklist

Wireframes

To deliver faster reducing the cost of mistakes I started with wireframes for all core flows.

I designed a sufficient amount of screens demanded to demonstrate the future user experience and the live interaction model.

Deliverables:
  • Sketches
  • Wireframes
  • Notes and Votes

Moodboard

To execute the right visual solution and satisfy both stakeholders and users I did visual research. By collecting information about their preferences and perception I created the Moodboard.

It was quite big with some alternative visual direction. During feedback sessions, I narrowed the final stack to a reasonable set.

Deliverables:
  • Moodboard
  • Color palette
  • Typography set

Product Mockups

I've designed a sufficient amount of mockups, covering all the needed flows and possible states. Using the inVision and Zeplin development team was able to build a live product using my designs.

Deliverables:
  • Mockups for Desktop
  • Mockups for Tablet
  • Guidelines
  • Animations
  • Interactive inVision Prototype
  • Zeplin uploads
